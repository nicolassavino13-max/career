# Why Dubai / UAE

> Answers for "Why Dubai?", "Are you open to relocating to Dubai?", "Why the Middle East?", "Why UAE?"

---

## Core answer (any Dubai role)

> Dubai is one of the most relevant fintech hubs globally right now — the regulatory environment (DIFC, ADGM), the concentration of global companies, the payments and crypto infrastructure buildout. It's also where several companies I'm most excited about operate from: Revolut, Checkout.com, and others are running meaningful regional or global operations out of Dubai. The compensation structure and tax environment make it genuinely attractive for someone at this stage of a career. It's a deliberate target, not a fallback.

---

## Why Dubai over other global hubs

> London is expensive and post-Brexit has lost some of its fintech density. Singapore is attractive but further from my network. Europe is accessible with my Italian passport but slower-moving. Dubai has the combination of: high-growth fintech market, global talent concentration, USD/tax-efficient compensation, and regulatory infrastructure that supports the payments and crypto space. The risk/reward for a career move is strong.

---

## Are you open to relocating to Dubai?

> Yes — I've made this decision deliberately. Dubai is one of my primary targets. I'm not treating it as a backup to São Paulo. If the right role is in Dubai, I'm ready to move.

---

## DIFC / ADGM regulatory environment (for fintech roles)

- DIFC and ADGM are purpose-built financial free zones with English common law — relevant for understanding the regulatory context of fintech, payments, and crypto roles
- UAE has been actively building payments infrastructure and digital asset regulation
- CBUAE (Central Bank UAE) has a clear payments strategy — relevant for roles at companies navigating regional licensing

---

## If they ask "do you have existing connections or network in Dubai?"

> Not yet, though some colleagues from IESE and McKinsey have moved through the region. Part of the logic for the move is to build the Dubai network from a position of strength — coming in through a strong company rather than arriving cold.

---

## Handling "why would you leave Brazil for Dubai?" 

> The question I ask myself is: which move builds the most globally transferable career? Dubai puts me in proximity to global companies, USD compensation, and a fintech ecosystem that's growing faster than any market in Europe or LatAm except perhaps Brazil itself. I'm Brazilian, I have Italian citizenship, I'm trilingual — geographic optionality is a real asset for me and I want to use it.

---

## Compensation framing for Dubai roles

> USD 10k+ net per month is my target for a Dubai role. That's realistic at Manager/Sr Manager level in fintech in the UAE. The tax efficiency makes it significantly better than an equivalent gross in Brazil or Europe.

---

## If asked "what do you know about the UAE/MENA market?"

> I know the regional fintech landscape at a macro level — DIFC, ADGM, CBUAE's payments strategy, the concentration of global fintechs in Dubai vs Abu Dhabi, the role of the UAE in regional cross-border payments (remittances, B2B, crypto rails). I don't have direct on-the-ground experience in the market yet — that's part of what makes joining [company] at this moment interesting: I'd be building that local market knowledge from a strong company platform.
