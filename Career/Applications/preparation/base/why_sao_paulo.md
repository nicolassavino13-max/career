# Why São Paulo

> Answers for "Why São Paulo?", "Are you open to SP?", "Where do you want to be based?", "Would you relocate?"

---

## Core answer (any SP role)

> São Paulo is the right market for this move. It's the biggest fintech and financial services ecosystem in Latin America — Mercado Pago, Nubank, Adyen's LatAm hub, acquirers, neobanks. If I'm going to build operator credibility in fintech, São Paulo is where that happens in Brazil. It's also where I can have real impact fast.

---

## When asked "are you already in SP or would you need to relocate?"

> I'm currently making this move deliberately — São Paulo is where the ecosystem I want to be in is concentrated. I don't need time to decide; this is the plan.

---

## São Paulo as a launchpad (honest framing)

> The honest answer is I see São Paulo as a launchpad rather than a final destination. The goal is to build the strongest possible operator profile in fintech — and then use that to access global roles in Dubai, remote/global, or European fintech companies. But the first move needs to be in a strong market with a real ecosystem. SP is that market.
>
> ⚠ **Use this framing carefully.** Only when the company culture supports global ambition and you trust the interviewer. With most SP companies, lead with "SP is the right market" and hold the launchpad framing for later stages.

---

## São Paulo-specific context

- Faria Lima / Vila Olímpia / Paulista are the financial hubs — familiar with the geography and culture
- Presencial / hybrid culture in Brazilian financial services: manageable if role scope is right
- LatAm fintech ecosystem: Nubank, Mercado Pago, Adyen SP, Stone, EBANX, dLocal — best talent concentration for this career phase
- CFA III and McKinsey brand travel well within the Brazilian financial market

---

## If asked about work-life balance / culture fit in SP

> I worked in consulting, which is demanding by design. São Paulo's high-performance culture is something I know and can navigate. The line I draw is sustainable intensity vs permanent grind — I want to build something over years, not sprint until I burn out. If the team operates in that 45–55h range, that works well for me.

---

## If asked "but you have a Dubai opportunity — why São Paulo?"

> Both are real options for me. The decision is about which role and which company, not just geography. São Paulo has the advantage of LatAm network depth and being in the same ecosystem I've been advising. Dubai has the advantage of USD compensation and global company access. For the right role, either works.
