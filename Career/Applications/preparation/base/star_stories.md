## S1 — Digital Wallet MVP (6 Central American Markets)

**Headline:** Shaped the business case and go-to-market strategy for a bank-backed digital wallet, from concept to live product across 6 markets in ~2 months.

**When to use:**
- "Tell me about a time you worked on a digital product / fintech build"
- "What fintech experience do you bring?"
- "Tell me about execution under time pressure"
- Most fintech platform, digital banking, or growth roles

**Opening line:**
> "One of the projects I'm most proud of was helping take a bank-backed digital wallet from idea to launched product across 6 markets in under two months."

---

**SITUATION**
A traditional bank in Central America wanted to compete with digital-first challengers by launching a standalone digital wallet. The competitive window was narrow — the bank needed to move at fintech speed, not bank speed. The wallet needed to launch outside the parent bank's structure to avoid bureaucratic drag.

**TASK**
My role was to build the financial foundation and shape the product strategy: unit economics model, value proposition, product roadmap, and the lean operating model for the fintech entity.

**ACTION**
- Built the end-to-end financial model — unit economics, customer acquisition cost, P&L scenarios across 6 markets.
- Shaped the product roadmap, sequencing features for fastest viable launch rather than full feature parity.
- Designed the operating structure as an independent fintech entity — separated from the parent bank to allow faster iteration.
- Worked across the client team, external partners, and country leads to align on the launch plan.

**RESULT**
The wallet MVP went live in approximately 2 months across 6 Central American markets. The structure we built allowed the bank to operate at fintech speed without the parent bank's approval cycles.

**Key numbers:** 6 markets, ~2-month launch, standalone fintech structure
**⚠ Assumption:** Exact timeline (~2 months) and market count (6) sourced from career context — confirm before use.

## S2 — LatAm Stablecoin Cross-Border Payments Consortium

**Headline:** Designed the full strategic plan for a multi-bank LatAm consortium to launch a USD-pegged stablecoin network for cross-border payments.

**When to use:**
- "What's your crypto / stablecoin experience?"
- "Tell me about your payments infrastructure work"
- "Have you worked on ecosystem or multi-party strategy?"
- Crypto infra, payments rails, consortium/partnership strategy, Revolut EMEA, Circle, Ripple-type roles

**Opening line:**
> "The most forward-looking payments project I worked on was helping a group of LatAm banks design a stablecoin-based network to replace correspondent banking for cross-border payments."

---

**SITUATION**
A group of Latin American banks were frustrated by the cost, speed and opacity of SWIFT-based correspondent banking for intra-LatAm transfers. They wanted to explore blockchain-based infrastructure but didn't know how to structure a shared model.

**TASK**
Build the complete strategic framework for a multi-bank consortium launching a USD-pegged stablecoin for cross-border payments: operating model, regulatory path, market entry sequencing, and governance structure.

**ACTION**
- Mapped the regulatory landscape across target LatAm markets — what was permissible, what required lobbying, what could launch now vs later.
- Designed the consortium operating model: governance rules, membership contribution structure, revenue sharing, exit provisions.
- Built the market entry strategy with country sequencing based on regulatory readiness and bank coverage.
- Analyzed competitor models (SWIFT alternatives, other blockchain payment consortia) to stress-test the design.

**RESULT**
Delivered a complete strategic and operational blueprint for the consortium — regulatory path, operating model, and a sequenced launch plan. This became the framework the banks used to advance the project internally.

**Key numbers:** Multi-bank consortium, LatAm, USD-pegged stablecoin
**⚠ Assumption:** Number of participating banks and exact countries not confirmed in career context — do not cite specifics without verifying.

- Led executive working sessions to align the leadership team on priorities and trade-offs.
- Stress-tested initiatives against volatility scenarios to ensure resilience.

**RESULT**
A board-approved 4-year growth strategy with executive alignment, scenario-resilient prioritization, and a clear initiative roadmap.

**Key numbers:** 4-year strategy, one of the largest retail banks in DR

## S5 — USD 144M Marine Conservation Finance Program

**When to use:**
- "Tell me about managing complex stakeholder environments"
- "Describe a situation where you had to align groups with very different interests"
- Partnerships, ecosystems, corp dev, multi-party programs
- Questions about operating in ambiguous, multi-stakeholder contexts

**Opening line:**
> "When people ask me about managing stakeholder complexity, I go to a conservation finance project — USD 144 million, with government agencies, international donors, NGOs, and local communities all needing to agree."

---

**SITUATION**
A USD 144M marine conservation finance initiative was struggling to close. The challenge wasn't funding — it was governance: multiple stakeholders (government entities, international donors, NGOs, community groups) had conflicting timelines, interests, and accountability requirements.

**TASK**
Build an execution roadmap and governance structure that would enable the program to close formally — meeting each stakeholder group's requirements while maintaining forward momentum.

**ACTION**
- Mapped the full stakeholder landscape: who had approval rights, who had veto power, who needed what documentation.
- Sequenced deliverables to unblock the critical path — identified which governance requirements had to be met before others could move.
- Facilitated multi-party alignment sessions with groups that had not been in the same room before.
- Developed governance documentation (mitigation sizing, program closing requirements) to satisfy donor and government requirements.

**RESULT**
Execution roadmap completed, enabling the program to advance toward formal closing and disbursement.

**Key numbers:** USD 144M, multi-country, government + NGO + donors
**⚠ Assumption:** Final program closure status and exact number of stakeholder groups not confirmed — use directionally.

## S6 — Petrobras Merchant Navy: Crew Leadership + International Asset Sale

**Headline:** Led vessel operations as Captain at Petrobras, including a 15-person multinational crew and managing an operational knowledge transfer to an international Asian buyer during a vessel sale.

**When to use:**
- "Tell me about a leadership challenge"
- "Describe working in a cross-cultural or high-stakes environment"
- "What makes you different from a typical consultant?"
- Establishing operator credibility before consulting career; leadership under pressure

**Opening line:**
> "Before consulting, I was a Captain in the Brazilian Merchant Navy — and the experience that shaped how I think about execution was when we were selling the vessel to an Asian buyer and I had to manage the entire operational handover cross-culturally."

---

**SITUATION**
I was commanding a vessel at Petrobras with a 15-person multinational crew, managing safety-critical operations. When Petrobras decided to sell the asset, I was asked to manage the full operational handover to an international Asian buyer who operated under completely different norms.

**TASK**
Maintain zero-incident vessel operations while managing the complex cross-cultural knowledge transfer — including documentation, technical demonstrations, and crew coordination with buyers who didn't share the same language or operational standards.

**ACTION**
- Led crew management including conflict resolution, code of conduct training, PPE and supply management.
- During the asset sale: served as the primary operational contact for the buyer team, managing documentation handover, operational demos, and safety briefings.
- Adapted communication style significantly — operating in multilingual settings with limited shared language.
- Maintained full operational safety standards throughout the transition period.

**RESULT**
Zero incidents during tenure. Successful handover completed to the international buyer.

**Key numbers:** 15-person crew, multinational environment, successful asset sale handover
**⚠ Assumption:** Specific vessel type and buyer name confidential; zero-incident claim based on career context.
